## Why it's in the library

- **Through-composition.** Almost no spoken book — the contour is nearly continuous. Shows a user
  what a sung-through density looks like next to Fiddler's song-and-scene alternation.
- **Motif density is the lesson.** The map above is the point: a melody or phrase planted early
  returns transformed, and the finale (#46) is built almost entirely from earlier motifs. This is
  the reference a user opens to understand reprise and leitmotif.
- **The divergence exemplar.** "It's Quiet Uptown" (#41) sits at tension 8 / energy 2 — the
  whisper-quiet gut-punch the dual contour's divergence band exists to surface.
- **Delayed payoff.** The "shot" motif (#3) isn't resolved until the duel (#45), 42 numbers
  later — long-range structural setup worth studying.

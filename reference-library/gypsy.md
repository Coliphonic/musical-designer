## Why it's in the library

- **The 11 o'clock number, defined.** "Rose's Turn" is the slot's namesake achievement: a late
  solo where the protagonist's whole psychology erupts. Tension peaks at 10 while energy stays
  moderate — a star turn, not a production number — exactly the divergence the dual contour shows.
- **A motif that carries the arc.** "Let Me Entertain You" appears as a children's act (#1),
  then as Louise's strip (#15) — same tune, opposite meaning. The cleanest single-motif
  transformation in the canon; a model for the `Motif` entity's meaning-states.
- **Act 1 finale as engine, not climax.** "Everything's Coming Up Roses" sends the audience out
  on Rose's terrifying resolve, not a plot cliffhanger — a character-driven act button to
  contrast with Wicked's spectacle finale.

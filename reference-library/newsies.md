## Why it's in the library

- **Anthem engine.** Where Fiddler builds on tradition and ritual, Newsies runs on *rallying
  cries* — "The World Will Know," "Seize the Day," "Once and for All" are escalating anthems.
  A user writing a movement/uprising show can study how the anthems are spaced so they don't
  blunt each other.
- **Diegetic island.** "That's Rich" and the Bowery Beauties in "I Never Planned on You" are
  performed in-world inside Medda's theater — the cleanest contemporary example of the diegetic
  vs. book-song distinction (the `diegetic` card flag).
- **Textbook high-energy/low-tension number.** "King of New York" opens Act 2 as pure release
  (energy 9, tension 4) — the divergence the dual contour is built to make visible, right next
  to "Letter from the Refuge" (energy 3, tension 6) one slot later.
- **Delayed I Want.** "Santa Fe" is teased in the prologue (#1) and only fully paid off at the
  Act 1 finale (#9) — a structural choice worth contrasting with Little Mermaid's early,
  complete "Part of Your World."
